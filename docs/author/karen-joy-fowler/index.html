Author: Karen Joy Fowler

Books

We Are All Completely Beside Ourselves 2014

Karen Joy Fowler is a celebrated American author known for her diverse range of works that span genres such as science fiction, fantasy, and contemporary fiction. Born in Bloomington, Indiana, Fowler grew up in the college town of Palo Alto, California. She attended the University of California, Berkeley, where she studied political science, and also holds an MA in creative writing from the University of California, Davis.

Fowler's career took off with her debut novel, "Sarah Canary," published in 1991, which blends historical fiction with speculative elements. Her keen sense of psychological insight and narrative ingenuity has earned her a dedicated following. She gained widespread acclaim with "The Jane Austen Book Club" (2004), a novel that intertwines the lives of its characters with the timeless works of Austen, which was later adapted into a film.

Throughout her career, Fowler has received numerous awards, including the PEN/Faulkner Award for Fiction in 2014 for "We Are All Completely Beside Ourselves." Her works often explore themes of family, identity, and the complex intricacies of human relationships, establishing her as a distinctive voice in contemporary literature.
