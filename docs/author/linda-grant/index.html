Linda Grant is a celebrated British author known for her insightful explorations of history, identity, and the immigrant experience. Born in Liverpool in 1951 to Russian and Polish Jewish parents, Grant's heritage deeply influences her writing. She studied English at the University of York and pursued further studies in Canada, enriching her understanding of diverse cultures and narratives.

Grant began her career as a journalist, contributing to numerous publications, before transitioning to fiction writing. Her debut novel, "The Cast Iron Shore" (1995), immediately garnered attention for its compelling portrayal of post-war Britain. Grant's subsequent works, including "When I Lived in Modern Times" and "The Clothes on Their Backs," have been lauded for their lyrical prose and profound thematic depth.

Renowned for her ability to weave personal and historical threads, Grant has carved a unique niche in contemporary literature. Her novels often examine the complexities of identity and the passage of time, resonating with readers worldwide. Alongside her fiction, she has also penned notable non-fiction works, further cementing her status as a versatile and influential literary voice.
