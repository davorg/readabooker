Author: Joshua Ferris

Joshua Ferris is an acclaimed American author known for his insightful exploration of contemporary life and the human condition. Born on November 8, 1974, in Danville, Illinois, he grew up in Key West, Florida, and later pursued his education at the University of Iowa, earning a Bachelor of Arts in English and Philosophy. Ferris further honed his craft by obtaining a Master of Fine Arts degree in Creative Writing from the University of California, Irvine.

Ferris's debut novel, *Then We Came to the End* (2007), garnered widespread praise for its poignant and darkly comic portrayal of office culture. The novel was shortlisted for the National Book Award and won the PEN/Hemingway Award. His subsequent works, including *The Unnamed* (2010) and *To Rise Again at a Decent Hour* (2014), solidified his reputation as a writer adept at capturing the complexities of modern existence with wit and empathy. Ferris's writing often delves into themes of identity, alienation, and the search for meaning, resonating deeply with readers around the world.
