Biography

Indra Sinha is a distinguished British-Indian author and former advertising copywriter renowned for his poignant storytelling and deep engagement with social issues. Born in 1950 in Colaba, Mumbai, Sinha spent his early years in India before moving to England for education at a boarding school and later attending Cambridge University. His multicultural background and life experiences have significantly influenced his literary voice.

Sinha initially made a name for himself in the advertising world but eventually transitioned to full-time writing. His works often reflect his dedication to human rights and activism, most notably demonstrated in his acclaimed novel "Animal's People," which has garnered critical acclaim for its raw depiction of the long-term effects of industrial disaster on a community in India.

In addition to his fiction, Sinha has contributed to various humanitarian causes, including advocating for victims of the 1984 Bhopal disaster, drawing international attention through his writing. He continues to be celebrated for intertwining compelling narratives with pressing global issues, making a lasting impact both within and beyond literary circles.
