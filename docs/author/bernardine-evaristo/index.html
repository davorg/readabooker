Biography

Bernardine Evaristo is an acclaimed British author celebrated for her innovative narrative style and exploration of the African diaspora's diverse stories. Born in 1959 to an English mother and Nigerian father, Evaristo grew up in Woolwich, South London. She studied at the Rose Bruford College of Speech and Drama, where she honed her creative skills.

Evaristo's literary career is marked by her commitment to spotlighting marginalized voices. Her works often blend poetry and prose, and she has authored several novels, including "The Emperor's Babe," "Blonde Roots," and "Mr. Loverman." Her groundbreaking approach to storytelling, often characterized by a fusion of historical and contemporary themes, has earned her widespread critical acclaim.

In addition to her writing, Evaristo is a prominent advocate for diversity in the arts, co-founding the Theatre of Black Women, the UK's first such company, in 1982. She also tackles issues of race, gender, and sexuality both in her writing and through her active participation in various literary and cultural organizations. Through her impressive body of work and advocacy, Evaristo continues to inspire and influence the literary landscape globally.
