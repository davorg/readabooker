Biography

Madeleine St John was an acclaimed Australian author born on November 12, 1941, in Sydney, Australia. She was raised in a creative environment, her father being renowned journalist Edward St John. After attending the University of Sydney, where she mingled with notable figures including future writers such as Clive James and Germaine Greer, she moved to the United Kingdom in the late 1960s.

St John published her first novel, "The Women in Black," in 1993. The novel, set in a 1950s Sydney department store, was praised for its wit and insightful depiction of the everyday lives of its female characters. She followed it with several more novels, including "A Pure Clear Light" and "The Essence of the Thing," which showcased her keen eye for human relationships and social nuances.

Although she spent much of her life in the UK, St John's works often referenced her Australian roots, highlighting her unique narrative voice. She passed away on June 18, 2006, leaving behind a legacy celebrated for its sharp humor, emotional depth, and cultural insight.
