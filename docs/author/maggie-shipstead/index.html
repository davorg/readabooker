Maggie Shipstead is an acclaimed American author known for her vivid storytelling and keen insight into the human condition. Born in 1983 in Orange County, California, Shipstead grew up with a love for reading and writing, later pursuing her passion for literature at Harvard University, where she graduated with a degree in English. She further honed her craft at the Iowa Writers' Workshop, one of the most prestigious writing programs in the United States.

Shipstead's debut novel, "Seating Arrangements," published in 2012, garnered critical acclaim and established her as a fresh voice in contemporary fiction. Her subsequent works, including "Astonish Me" and "Great Circle," have continued to captivate readers with their intricate plots and richly drawn characters. Shipstead's novels often explore themes of identity, ambition, and the complexities of human relationships.

In addition to her novels, Shipstead's essays and short stories have appeared in numerous esteemed publications, showcasing her versatility as a writer. Her work has earned her several accolades, recognizing her as one of the prominent literary talents of her generation. Maggie Shipstead currently resides in Los Angeles, where she continues to write and inspire readers worldwide.
