Maaza Mengiste is an acclaimed Ethiopian-American author known for her rich narratives and exploration of historical themes, particularly centered around Ethiopia. Born in Addis Ababa, Mengiste grew up during the Ethiopian Revolution, an experience that profoundly influences her literary work. She later moved to the United States, where she pursued her education, eventually earning an MFA in Creative Writing from New York University.

Mengiste's debut novel, *Beneath the Lionâ€™s Gaze* (2010), received critical acclaim for its vivid depiction of the Ethiopian Revolution's impact on families. Her second novel, *The Shadow King* (2019), delves into the Italo-Ethiopian War and highlights the overlooked contributions of women in warfare. This work was shortlisted for several prestigious awards and praised for its lyrical prose and historical depth.

In addition to her novels, Mengiste's essays and articles have been featured in prominent publications such as *The New Yorker*, *Granta*, and *The New York Times*. She is recognized not only for her storytelling but also for her contributions to bringing African narratives to a global audience. Beyond her writing, Mengiste engages in public speaking and advocacy, focusing on issues related to immigration and African history.
