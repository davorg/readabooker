Author: Brandon Taylor

Books

Real Life 2020

Brandon Taylor is a critically acclaimed American writer known for his evocative storytelling and nuanced exploration of human relationships. Born in 1989 in Prattville, Alabama, Taylor first pursued a career in science, earning degrees in biochemistry and creative writing. His transition from science to literature marked the beginning of a remarkable literary journey.

Taylor's debut novel, "Real Life," published in 2020, garnered widespread praise for its insightful portrayal of a young Black queer man's experience in academia. The novel was shortlisted for the prestigious Booker Prize and earned Taylor a significant place in contemporary American literature. His writing is celebrated for its clarity, emotional depth, and exploration of themes such as race, identity, and belonging.

In addition to his novel, Taylor is also known for his short stories, many of which have appeared in esteemed publications like The New Yorker. His collection "Filthy Animals," released in 2021, further cemented his reputation as a master of the short story form. Taylor continues to captivate readers with his incisive and empathetic narratives.
