Biography

Avni Doshi is an American novelist renowned for her evocative prose and intricate storytelling. Born in 1982 in New Jersey to Indian parents, she pursued art history at Barnard College before earning a master's degree in the history of art from University College London. Doshi's multicultural upbringing heavily influences her writing, often exploring themes of identity, memory, and familial relationships.

Her critically acclaimed debut novel, "Burnt Sugar" (published as "Girl in White Cotton" in India), explores the complex, often fraught relationship between a mother and daughter. The novel's vivid exploration of personal and cultural tensions, combined with its sharp narrative style, has captivated readers worldwide. Doshi skillfully blends her art history background into her narratives, bringing a unique perspective to her literary work.

Currently residing in Dubai, Doshi continues to write and contribute to global literary conversations. Her work, which often challenges societal norms and delves into the nuances of human emotion, marks her as a distinctive voice in contemporary literature.
