Author: Emma Donoghue

Books

Room 2010

Emma Donoghue is an acclaimed Irish-Canadian author known for her richly imaginative and emotionally resonant storytelling. Born in Dublin, Ireland, in 1969, she grew up in a literary family, fostering a deep passion for writing from a young age. Donoghue pursued her studies at the University of Cambridge, where she earned a Ph.D. in English, further honing her expertise in literature.

Her breakthrough novel, "Room," published in 2010, brought her international fame, earning numerous awards and acclaim for its unique narrative style and powerful portrayal of a mother and child held captive. The novel was later adapted into a successful film, for which she penned the screenplay, earning an Academy Award nomination.

Equally skilled in historical fiction, Donoghue authored works such as "Slammerkin" and "The Wonder," delving into the lives and struggles of fascinating characters across different eras. Donoghue's writing often explores themes of love, identity, and survival, marked by meticulous research and vibrant character development. Residing in Canada, she continues to captivate readers worldwide with her diverse body of work, which spans novels, short stories, and plays.
