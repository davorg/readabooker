Richard Powers, born on June 18, 1957, in Evanston, Illinois, is a celebrated American author known for his intricate narratives and explorations of the intersection between science, technology, and the human experience. He was raised in both Illinois and Thailand, an experience that contributed to his broad worldview and eclectic interests. Powers initially pursued a career in physics but switched to literature when he realized his passion for storytelling.

He is renowned for his ability to weave complex scientific themes into rich, character-driven plots. Powers' breakthrough novel, "Three Farmers on Their Way to a Dance," was published in 1985 and set the tone for a series of critically acclaimed works, including "The Gold Bug Variations" and "Operation Wandering Soul." His 2006 novel, "The Echo Maker," won the National Book Award, showcasing his prowess in blending psychological depth with expansive ideas.

In 2018, his novel "The Overstory," a profound narrative about the relationship between humans and trees, won the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction and further cemented his status as a leading voice in American literature. Powers continues to captivate readers with his thought-provoking works and lives in the foothills of the Great Smoky Mountains.
