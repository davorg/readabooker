Biography

Katie Kitamura is a celebrated author and critic known for her incisive examination of human relationships and identity. Born to a family with roots in both the United States and Japan, Kitamura's multicultural background has significantly influenced her literary perspective. She studied English at Princeton University before earning her Masterâ€™s in Creative Writing at the London Consortium.

Kitamura's debut novel, "The Longshot" (2009), received critical acclaim for its gripping portrayal of the martial arts world. Her subsequent works, including "Gone to the Forest" (2012) and "A Separation" (2017), further established her as a compelling voice in contemporary fiction. Her most recent novel, "Intimacies" (2021), explores themes of communication, interpretation, and the subtleties of personal and political power dynamics.

In addition to her fiction, Kitamura's essays and reviews have appeared in renowned publications such as The New York Times, The Guardian, and Granta. With her keen insights and evocative storytelling, Kitamura continues to be a formidable presence in the literary world, captivating readers across the globe.
