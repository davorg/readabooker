Author: Chigozie Obioma

Books

The Fishermen 2015
An Orchestra of Minorities 2019

Chigozie Obioma is a Nigerian author born in Akure, Nigeria. His early life in Nigeria significantly influenced his writing, imbuing his work with rich cultural themes and narratives rooted in Igbo traditions. Obioma pursued studies in English and literature, earning a degree from Cyprus International University. He later moved to the United States to further his studies, completing an MFA in Creative Writing at the University of Michigan.

Obioma's literary career took off with his debut novel, "The Fishermen," which received critical acclaim for its vivid storytelling and exploration of familial bonds entwined with Nigerian folklore. His second novel, "An Orchestra of Minorities," further established his reputation as a compelling voice in contemporary literature. Both novels have been translated into multiple languages, expanding his readership globally.

Beyond his novels, Obioma's essays and short stories have appeared in prestigious publications, showcasing his versatility as a writer. He currently serves as a professor of English and Creative Writing at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, where he continues to inspire a new generation of writers. Chigozie Obioma remains a significant figure in the literary world, celebrated for his contribution to modern storytelling and cultural exploration.
