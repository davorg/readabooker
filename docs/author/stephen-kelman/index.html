Author: Stephen Kelman

Stephen Kelman is a distinguished British author, best known for his debut novel "Pigeon English," published in 2011. Born in Luton in 1976, Kelman grew up with a deep interest in storytelling, a passion that would later shape his writing career. He studied marketing at the University of Bedfordshire before venturing into various jobs, all the while nurturing his literary ambitions.

"Pigeon English" draws inspiration from real-life events and his own experiences, offering a raw and poignant look at childhood and gang culture through the eyes of an eleven-year-old Ghanaian immigrant in London. This novel garnered critical acclaim for its unique voice and vivid portrayal of inner-city life.

Kelman's writing is celebrated for its authenticity, emotional depth, and ability to tackle complex social issues with sensitivity and nuance. Beyond "Pigeon English," he continues to contribute to contemporary literature with works that reflect his keen observational skills and commitment to capturing life's multifaceted realities. Through his compelling narratives, Kelman remains a significant figure in the literary landscape, known for his insightful exploration of human experiences.
