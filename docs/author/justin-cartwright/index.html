Justin Cartwright was a distinguished British author known for his insightful and poignant narratives. Born in Cape Town, South Africa, in 1943, he moved to England where he cultivated a rich literary career. Cartwright studied at the University of Oxford and began his professional life in advertising and film before fully dedicating himself to writing.

He published numerous critically acclaimed novels, with standout works such as "In Every Face I Meet," which was shortlisted for the Whitbread Novel Award, and "Leading the Cheers," which won the Whitbread Novel Award in 1998. His writing is often characterized by its exploration of complex themes such as identity, history, and the human condition, delivered through carefully crafted, multidimensional characters.

Cartwright's deeply reflective style and ability to capture the subtleties of life garnered him a dedicated readership. His contributions to literature continued until his passing in 2018, leaving behind a legacy of profound and resonant works that continue to engage and inspire readers worldwide.
