Biography

Damon Galgut is a celebrated South African novelist and playwright, known for his profound and intricate exploration of human relationships and the socio-political landscapes of his homeland. Born in Pretoria on November 12, 1963, Galgut experienced a significant challenge early in life when he was diagnosed with cancer at the age of six, an ordeal that had a lasting impact on his worldview and later writing.

Educated at the University of Cape Town, Galgut published his first novel, *A Sinless Season*, at the age of 17. Over the years, his novels have often grappled with themes of displacement, existential struggle, and the complex dynamics of South African society. His notable works include *The Good Doctor*, *The Impostor*, and *In a Strange Room*.

Galgut's writing is acclaimed for its spare, evocative style and its deep psychological insight. Alongside his novels, he has also written several plays. Residing in Cape Town, Galgut's literary contributions continue to captivate a global audience, securing his place as one of contemporary literature's most vital voices.
