Arundhati Roy is an Indian author and activist renowned for her profound literary contributions and vocal advocacy on social and political issues. Born on November 24, 1961, in Shillong, India, she grew up in Kerala. Roy gained international acclaim with her debut novel, "The God of Small Things," published in 1997. The novel, a semi-autobiographical work, intricately explores themes of love, loss, and social discrimination within the context of a traditional Indian family.

Beyond fiction, Roy is a passionate political activist, engaging in environmental causes, anti-globalization movements, and advocating for human rights. Her collection of essays, such as "The Algebra of Infinite Justice" (2002) and "Field Notes on Democracy" (2009), reflects her commitment to exploring these complex issues with incisive clarity.

Roy's distinctive voice and courage in addressing contentious topics have made her a prominent figure not only in literature but also in global socio-political discourse. Her works continue to inspire readers and provoke thoughtful dialogue on the myriad challenges facing contemporary society.
