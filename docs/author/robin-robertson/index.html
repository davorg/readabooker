Robin Robertson is a renowned Scottish poet and author, celebrated for his evocative and powerful use of language. Born in Scone, Perthshire, in 1955, Robertson grew up on the northeast coast of Scotland, a landscape that would profoundly influence his work. He studied at the University of Edinburgh, where he developed a deep appreciation for literature and classical studies.

Robertson's poetry is known for its vivid imagery and exploration of themes such as nature, mythology, and the intricacies of human experience. His debut collection, "A Painted Field," was published in 1997 and won several accolades. He followed this success with critically acclaimed collections like "Slow Air" and "Swithering."

In addition to his poetry, Robertson has worked as an editor and translator, bringing works from ancient Greek and modern European languages to English-speaking audiences. His narrative prose has also received recognition, with his 2018 verse novel, "The Long Take," earning widespread praise for its innovative style and emotional depth. Robertson's contributions to literature have solidified his reputation as a significant voice in contemporary writing.
