Biography

William Trevor, born William Trevor Cox on May 24, 1928, in Mitchelstown, County Cork, Ireland, was an esteemed novelist, playwright, and short story writer. Renowned for his poignant literary style, Trevor's works often explore themes of marginalized lives, unrequited love, and the quiet struggles of everyday existence. Educated at Trinity College Dublin, he initially pursued a career in sculpture before turning to writing, which would lead to a prolific career spanning over five decades.

Trevor's debut novel, "A Standard of Behaviour," was published in 1958, but it was with his short stories and subsequent novels like "The Old Boys" and "The Children of Dynmouth" that he gained notable acclaim. His writing garnered numerous awards, and he was frequently praised for his deft characterizations and his ability to capture the intricacies of human nature.

Residing in Devon, England, for much of his life, Trevor was a private individual who shied away from the spotlight, allowing his literary work to communicate his profound insights into the human condition. He passed away on November 20, 2016, leaving behind a legacy of richly evocative narratives.
