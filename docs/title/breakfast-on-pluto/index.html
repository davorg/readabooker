Book: Breakfast on Pluto

Patrick McCabe 1998
Buy on Amazon

"Breakfast on Pluto" by Patrick McCabe is a darkly comedic and poignant novel that delves into the life of Patrick "Pussy" Braden, a trans woman navigating the tumultuous landscape of 1970s and 1980s Ireland. Abandoned as a baby in the small town of Tyreelin, Patrick grows up in a challenging environment, facing societal prejudice while longing for her own identity. With wit and resilience, she embarks on a journey to London in search of her estranged mother, encountering a motley crew of characters and unsettling situations along the way. McCabe's novel vividly captures Patrick's voice, balancing her internal struggles with raw humor and empathy. Through a series of vignettes and diary entries, the book paints a layered portrait of identity, solitude, and the yearning for acceptance amidst the backdrop of a politically charged era. "Breakfast on Pluto" is a daring exploration of the human spirit, celebrating the indomitable courage it takes to live one's truth.
