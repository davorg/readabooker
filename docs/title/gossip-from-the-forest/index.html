Book: Gossip from the Forest

Thomas Keneally

Shortlisted 1975

Description

"Gossip from the Forest" by Thomas Keneally is a compelling historical novel that delves into the intricate dynamics of war and peace. Set in the closing days of World War I, the narrative centers on the tense and meticulous negotiations for the Armistice in a forest in Compiègne, France. Keneally masterfully portrays the contrasting personalities and motivations of the key historical figures involved, including German and Allied representatives. Through rich characterization and vivid dialogue, the novel explores themes of human frailty, the burdens of leadership, and the moral complexities of brokering peace in a world ravaged by conflict. As the discussions unfold over a span of three days, Keneally captures the atmospheric tension and the weight of history pressing upon the negotiators, highlighting both the pettiness and nobility of human nature. The forest, a symbol of both concealment and revelation, serves as an evocative backdrop to the story, underscoring the novel's examination of the thin veneer of civilization placed over the raw forces of human ambition and despair. "Gossip from the Forest" is a thought-provoking reflection on the nature of peace and the human cost of diplomacy.
