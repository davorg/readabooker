The Story of Lucy Gault Such a Long Journey

Book: Study for Obedience

Sarah Bernstein

Shortlisted 2023

Description

"Study for Obedience" by Sarah Bernstein is a thought-provoking exploration of identity, compliance, and the haunting legacy of history. The novel centers around a nameless narrator who relocates to an unnamed, isolated Northern village to care for her brother after a personal upheaval. The locals, wary of outsiders, are further agitated by a series of uncanny occurrences coinciding with her arrival. As she struggles to fit into the tightly-knit community, she reflects on themes of servitude and the complexities of familial duty.

Bernstein's narrative, both spare and compelling, delves into the psyche of a woman navigating the often oppressive confines of societal expectation. Her protagonist's introspective journey reveals the tensions between acceptance and alienation, highlighting the universal human desire for belonging amidst ubiquitous prejudices. "Study for Obedience" challenges readers to consider the intricate dance between individuality and subservience, ultimately posing questions about the cost of compliance in a world eager to enforce conformity. Through vivid prose and a haunting atmosphere, Bernstein crafts a novel that remains with readers long after the final page.
