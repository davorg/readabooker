The 27th Kingdom

Book: 10 Minutes 38 Seconds in This Strange World

Elif Shafak

Shortlisted 2019

Description

"10 Minutes 38 Seconds in This Strange World" by Elif Shafak is a profoundly moving novel that delves into the complexities of memory, friendship, and the societal undercurrents of Istanbul. The book begins with the end of Leila's life, as her consciousness lingers for a brief period following her death. During these ten minutes and thirty-eight seconds, Leila's mind flickers through vivid memories that define her life, transporting readers through her tumultuous journey marked by family estrangement, exploitation, and a resilient pursuit of freedom. As each second unfolds, Leila revisits moments filled with the warmth and loyalty of the eclectic group of outcasts she calls friends, who form her true family in the vibrant, often unforgiving environment of Istanbul. Through Leila’s story, Shafak weaves a rich tapestry of the city’s beauty and darkness, offering a deeply empathetic exploration of identity and belonging in the margins of society. This poignant narrative challenges readers to reflect on the enduring impact of love and connection in the face of adversity.
