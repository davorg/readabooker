Book: God on the Rocks

Jane Gardam

Shortlisted 1978

Description

"God on the Rocks" by Jane Gardam is a compelling coming-of-age tale set against the backdrop of 1930s England. The narrative centers around eight-year-old Margaret Marsh, whose life is steeped in the rigidities of her religious upbringing. Her father, a fervent member of a Christian sect, instills in her a sense of spiritual duty that becomes increasingly at odds with the quirky and colorful world unfolding around her.

Margaret's perceptions start to shift during an eventful summer when she forms an unlikely friendship with the free-spirited Lydia, her family's new housemaid. This relationship, paired with her encounters with an assortment of eccentric local characters, propels Margaret into a new understanding of life's complexities and contradictions.

Jane Gardam masterfully weaves themes of faith, family dynamics, and personal awakening with humor and poignancy. Her deft characterizations bring the inhabitants of this small coastal town to vivid life, exploring how the interplay between the restrictive forces of tradition and the promise of change can shape one's journey to self-awareness.
