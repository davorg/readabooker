Book: Saville

David Storey 1976
Buy on Amazon

"**Saville**" by David Storey is a powerful and introspective novel that delves into the life of Colin Saville, an intelligent and ambitious boy growing up in the industrial landscape of post-World War II Yorkshire. The novel weaves a poignant tale of self-discovery and resilience as Colin navigates the challenges of his working-class upbringing. He is caught between the traditional expectations of his family and the tantalizing possibilities of a different future that education might afford him.

Storey expertly crafts a vivid portrait of the era, highlighting the societal changes and tensions that characterize the time. Colin's journey is marked by internal and external conflicts, as he strives to find his own identity amidst the expectations of his community. Richly detailed and deeply humane, "Saville" explores themes of class, aspiration, and the search for personal fulfillment, capturing the nuances of life in a changing world. The novel's profound emotional depth and keen insights into human nature ensure its place as a compelling and timeless literary work.
