Book: The Gathering

Anne Enright

Winner 2007

Description

"The Gathering" by Anne Enright is a poignant exploration of family, memory, and the intricate ways they intertwine. The novel centers around Veronica Hegarty, who delves into her family's complicated past following the unexpected death of her brother, Liam. As Veronica grapples with her grief, she becomes the unofficial historian of the Hegarty family, unraveling layers of personal and familial history.

The narrative oscillates between Veronica's present-day reflections and her vivid recollections of her family's past, particularly a traumatic event that has been buried in her memory. Enright's prose is both lyrical and unflinching, bringing to light the raw emotions and hidden truths that define the Hegartys. The book delves into themes of love, loss, and the lasting impact of childhood events on adult life. With a mixture of stark realism and deep psychological insight, "The Gathering" captures the enduring bonds of family and the shadows that linger in their wake.
