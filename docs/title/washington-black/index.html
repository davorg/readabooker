Book: Washington Black

Esi Edugyan 2018
Buy on Amazon

"Washington Black" by Esi Edugyan is a sweeping historical adventure that explores themes of freedom, identity, and belonging through the eyes of its titular character, an eleven-year-old enslaved boy on a sugar plantation in Barbados. The narrative takes a dramatic turn when Washington, known as "Wash," is chosen to serve as the personal assistant to Christopher Wilde, an enigmatic and adventurous naturalist and inventor. Wilde, referred to as "Titch," introduces Wash to a world of science and exploration, sparking a journey that propels him across oceans and continents, from the icy Arctic to the bustling streets of London.

As Wash navigates his newfound freedom and Titch's complex motivations, he must confront the harsh realities of a world that views him as less than human. Edugyan masterfully blends historical fiction with elements of magical realism, creating a richly detailed and emotionally resonant story. "Washington Black" challenges readers to ponder deep questions about the nature of freedom, the enduring scars of slavery, and the lengths one will go to find a place in the world. Through Wash's journey, the novel paints a vivid portrait of resilience, courage, and the transformative power of friendship.
