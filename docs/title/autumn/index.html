Book: Autumn

Ali Smith 2017
Buy on Amazon

"Autumn" by Ali Smith is a poignant exploration of time, memory, and the transformative power of art and friendship. Set against the backdrop of a post-Brexit Britain, the novel weaves together the lives of Elisabeth, a young art history lecturer, and her centenarian friend, Daniel. Through a series of vivid, interconnected vignettes, Smith delves into themes of aging, identity, and the continuous passage of time. The narrative shifts seamlessly between the present and flashbacks to Daniel's past, revealing his rich tapestry of experiences and influence on Elisabeth's life. With prose that is both lyrical and sharp, Smith captures the mood of social uncertainty, contrasting it with the enduring beauty and resilience of human connections. "Autumn" challenges readers to reflect on the stories we tell and inherit, urging them to question the nature of truth and the role of art in a fractured world. The novel's inventive structure and playful language invite readers to savor each moment while contemplating the changing seasons of life.
